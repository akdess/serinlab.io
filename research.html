Research

Our lab develops integrative approaches to dissect the molecular landscape of brain tumors using single-cell and multi-modal data. We study how immune, neuron, and tumor cells interact, and we translate each patient’s molecular signature into tractable therapeutic hypotheses.

Single-cell atlases

Building large-scale single-cell profiles of tumors to define cell states, lineage programs, and resistance mechanisms.

Brain tumor microenvironments

Resolving cellular neighborhoods to understand tumor evolution, immune suppression, and treatment response.

Patient-specific targets

Identifying actionable genes, drug candidates, and pathways to guide individualized treatment.

We are especially interested in projects that connect method development with translational questions in neurosurgery and neuro-oncology.
